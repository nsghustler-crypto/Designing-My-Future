The Basics

What is Robotics?

Robotics is the branch of technology that deals with the design, construction, operation, and use of robots. But what exactly is a robot?

A Machine

At its core, a robot is a machine. Unlike a simple fan or a car, a robot can be programmed to perform tasks automatically.

Programmable

Robots follow instructions. We give them a "brain" (a computer chip) and tell them what to do using code.

Interactive

Robots sense the world (using sensors) and react to it. They don't just move; they respond to their environment.

1. Sensors (The Senses)

Just like our eyes and ears, robots use sensors to see light, feel touch, measure distance, or detect heat.

2. Controller (The Brain)

This is usually a microcontroller (like Arduino) or a computer. It processes the sensor data and makes decisions.

3. Actuators (The Muscles)

These are the motors, wheels, and arms. Once the brain decides, the actuators perform the physical action.

The Anatomy

How Do Robots Work?

Think of a robot like the human body. It needs to perceive the world, think about it, and then act upon it. This cycle is known as the Sense-Think-Act cycle.

  • Input devices gather data from surroundings.
  • Control systems process information using logic.
  • Output devices execute movements or tasks.
